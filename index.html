Our Approach

As researchers of the latest content creation and collaboration technologies, we approach workplace writing with a very broad definition of writing, that continues to expand. Our expertise ranges from preparing technical reports and proposals to navigating the social norms of the latest messaging apps.

Founded by a group of scholars of technical and professional writing, Their There Communication Consulting brings the latest research and evidence-based best practices in professional communication to your workplace. Rooted at the intersection of academia and industry, Their There Communication Consulting provides organizations with the skills and insights necessary to succeed in the modern workplace.

Services

Their There Communication Consulting offers virtual and in person training opportunities for individuals or large groups. We take the time to create customized solutions for each of our clients. First, we listen to your needs and, second, we design our programming as a direct response to your needs.

We create:

  • one-off workshops
  • new employee ‘boot camps’
  • virtual training programs
  • multi-session courses based on the needs of our clients

Some of our most popular topics include:

  • Audience Analysis: Understanding whom you’re writing to and how you fit in their life.
  • Knowing the Context: Determining the appropriate writing strategy for even the most challenging circumstances.
  • Better Writing Better Meetings: Developing best practices for communicating before, during, and after meetings to improve morale and maximize productivity.
  • Writing to Save Time: Figuring out how to say what needs to be said, no more, no less.
  • Overcoming Technical Difficulties: Focusing on using technology to make your team’s communication more effective.

Meet the Team

Ryan Wold, MA

Ryan’s research, teaching, and consulting all revolve around improving human connection. He currently teaches Business Writing Courses at the University of Minnesota where he is also pursuing his PhD in Rhetoric and Scientific and Technical Communication. His research focuses on how new technologies change the way people communicate. Ryan’s background as a retreat facilitator and experience in sales and marketing have made him a dynamic presenter. His workshops are entertaining, interactive, and leave participants prepared to overcome the real world challenges of business writing.

Brigitte Mussack, PhD

Brigitte’s research and work centers around uncovering and understanding shared values and working collaboratively with digital communication tools. She holds a PhD in Rhetoric and Scientific and Technical Communication from the University of Minnesota, where she currently teaches in the Department of Writing Studies and works as the Advanced Writing Coordinator. Her background work and research includes the areas of rhetorical theory and writing instruction, developing and facilitating professional development and instructor training, and learning transfer.

Jeremy Rosselot-Merritt, MTSC

Jeremy’s work focuses on the value of technical and professional communication in workplace settings and the relationship between industry and academic spaces. During his 14 years as a technical writer in industry, he wrote manuals and other documentation for clients in engineering, biopharmaceuticals, and other industries, while also conducting in-house training on writing and communication for direct colleagues. He holds a master’s degree in technical and scientific communication (MSTC) and is currently a PhD candidate in the University of Minnesota’s Rhetoric and Scientific and Technical Communication program.

Daniel Card, PhD

Dan’s research focuses on the tools and strategies that foster better communication in the face of complexity, risk, and uncertainty. He is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Writing Studies where he teaches courses on topics such as business writing, technical communication, information design, and digital rhetoric.
